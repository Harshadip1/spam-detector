Common Red Flags in Phishing Emails

Urgent Language

Phrases like "Act now!" or "Your account will be closed" create false urgency

Poor Grammar

Legitimate companies proofread their emails. Look for spelling and grammar errors

Suspicious Links

Hover over links to see if they lead to legitimate websites

Generic Greetings

"Dear Customer" instead of your actual name is often a red flag

Requests for Info

Legitimate companies never ask for passwords or sensitive info via email

Unexpected Emails

Be suspicious of emails from companies you don't have accounts with
